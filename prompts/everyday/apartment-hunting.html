Find an Apartment Without Getting Screwed Over

Looking for an apartment sucks. Between scam listings, hidden fees, terrible landlords, and that weird smell they swear will go away, it's a minefield. This prompt helps you know exactly what to look for, what questions to ask, and when to run away - before you sign anything.

The Prompt:

Why This Prompt Works:

Most people learn apartment hunting through expensive mistakes. This prompt gives you the knowledge of someone who's been burned before - what to check, what to ask, and what should make you walk away immediately.

It covers everything from spotting scams to documenting damage so you actually get your deposit back.

Example Input:

BUDGET: $1200/month max, have $3000 saved

LOCATION: Near downtown, 30-min commute max, near subway

MUST HAVES: Cat-friendly, laundry in building

NICE TO HAVES: Dishwasher, some sunlight

TIMELINE: Need to move in 6 weeks

SITUATION: Current roommate situation is toxic

CREDIT: Fair credit (650), stable job 2 years

HISTORY: Current place 2 years, good reference from previous

Apartment Hunting Survival Tips:

  • Never wire money: For anything. Ever. It's a scam
  • See it in person: Virtual tours hide everything bad
  • Check water pressure: Flush toilet while shower runs
  • Test cell signal: In every room, especially bedroom
  • Visit at night: See the real neighborhood
  • Google the landlord: Court records are public
  • Document everything: Every wall, floor, outlet, stain
  • Read entire lease: Yes, all 30 pages

Run Away If:

  • They want money before you see the place
  • "I'm out of town but my assistant will show you"
  • Lease doesn't match the ad
  • They're rushing you to sign
  • Multiple "application fees" to different companies
  • Won't give you a copy of the lease to review
  • Other tenants warn you
  • Black mold, bed bugs evidence, or weird smells
  • Landlord lives in the building (unless you want drama)

Questions That Reveal Everything:

  • "Why did the last tenant leave?"
  • "What's the average utility bill?"
  • "When was the last renovation?"
  • "How quickly do you handle maintenance?"
  • "What's your policy on guests?"
  • "Has there been any water damage?"
  • "Can I see the basement/utility room?"
  • "What company manages this building?"

Things You Can Negotiate:

  • Rent (especially if it's been listed 30+ days)
  • Security deposit payment plan
  • Pet deposits/fees
  • Parking spots
  • Paint colors
  • Lease length
  • Move-in date
  • Including utilities

Find Your Next Home (Not Your Next Nightmare)

A bad apartment can ruin your life for a year. Use this prompt to know exactly what to look for, what to avoid, and how to secure a place that won't make you miserable. Remember: If something feels off, it probably is. Trust your gut.

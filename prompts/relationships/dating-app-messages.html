Dating App Messages That Actually Get Responses

"Hey" gets ignored. "You're beautiful" is creepy. Pickup lines are cringe. So what DO you say? This prompt helps you write opening messages that stand out, start real conversations, and actually lead to dates. Works for Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, or any dating app.

The Prompt:

Why This Prompt Works:

Most dating app messages fail because they're either too generic ("hey beautiful") or trying too hard to be clever. This prompt creates messages that feel like starting a conversation with someone interesting at a party - natural, engaging, and specific to them.

The key is showing you actually looked at their profile (instant differentiation) while keeping things light and giving them something easy to respond to.

Example Input:

THE APP: Hinge

PHOTOS SHOW: Hiking, cooking, dog, travel (Japan), reading in coffee shop

BIO: "Therapist by day, terrible cook by night. My dog thinks I'm hilarious."

PROMPTS: "Simple pleasures" = Sunday farmers markets. "Never have I ever" = Been to Europe

MY STYLE: Playful and straightforward

MY GOAL: Relationship but starting casual

Message Strategy That Works:

  • Sunday evening = best time: Most active users, highest response rates
  • Question > Statement: Give them something to respond to
  • Specific > Generic: "Your Japan photos!" beats "Love travel"
  • Move fast: Suggest meeting within 10-15 messages
  • Two message rule: If they don't respond to two messages, unmatch and move on
  • Voice note test: If you wouldn't say it out loud, don't type it
  • Match their energy: Long responses to long, short to short

Conversation Timeline:

  • Messages 1-3: Opening and initial banter
  • Messages 4-7: Find common ground, share something about yourself
  • Messages 8-12: Suggest moving to text or meeting
  • After 15 messages: If no plans to meet, conversation usually dies

NEVER Send These:

  • "Hey" / "Hi" / "Hello beautiful"
  • Anything sexual or about their body
  • Copy-pasted pickup lines
  • "I don't usually do this but..."
  • Negative openers ("I hate this app")
  • Interview questions ("What do you do for work?")
  • Immediate date invites (message #1)
  • Long paragraphs about yourself
  • "Why are you single?"

For Different Profile Types:

  • Minimal profile: Comment on their best photo's location/activity
  • All group photos: Make a playful joke about which one they are
  • All selfies: Ask about their interests, suggest an activity
  • Travel heavy: Ask for recommendations, share similar experience
  • Dog/cat photos: Ask pet's name, don't make it whole conversation
  • Gym photos: Avoid fitness talk unless you're equally into it

Stand Out From 100 Other Matches

They probably have dozens of matches sending "hey" right now. Use this prompt to write messages they'll actually want to respond to. Remember: The goal isn't to be perfect - it's to start a conversation that leads somewhere.
